🔍 Rubrik — Customer Complaint Patterns
Aggregated from real user reviews and community posts across G2, Gartner Peer Insights, PeerSpot, Capterra, TrustRadius, and SoftwareAdvice over the past 12 months. Grouped by recurring themes — individual complaints are collapsed below each pattern. Use these patterns to inform discovery questions and objection handling in competitive deals.
Pricing Increases & Renewal Surprises Most cited pattern Pricing
▼

The most consistent complaint across all platforms. Users describe Rubrik as expensive relative to competitors, with pricing that increases significantly at renewal. Smaller organizations feel the pain most acutely. Multiple reviewers specifically call out that "price tends to go up with time" and that licensing complexity makes budgeting difficult. Some users report their local Rubrik sales team left and the relationship — and renewal negotiations — deteriorated afterward.

PeerSpot2025
"The solution would be better if it was cheaper and cost less to use. Price-wise, Rubrik needs improvement as it is considered expensive compared to similar competitors."

Support Responsiveness & Timezone Gaps Recurring pattern Support
▼

Users outside the US — particularly UK and APAC — report significant delays getting support, citing that tickets default to West Coast US hours, meaning a full business day delay before getting a response. One reviewer noted that "support is accurate 50-60% of the time." Product team availability also cited as an India-hours issue for international customers. Post-sale support quality described as inconsistent.

PeerSpot2025
"Support can be slow — we seem to get West Coast USA support if we log tickets from the UK. Takes a day to then get assistance."
peerspot.com/products/rubrik-reviews

Frequent UI Changes — Interface Instability Notable pattern UI / UX
▼

Multiple users describe confusion caused by Rubrik frequently changing layouts, menu structures, and terminology. This is particularly painful when returning to the product after time away — documentation no longer matches the interface, and restore workflows have moved or been renamed. Migration to Rubrik Security Cloud specifically called out as "tedious."

Reporting & Analytics Limitations Consistent feedback Reporting
▼

Users frequently cite limited reporting flexibility — columns can't be sorted, reports aren't customizable enough for audits, and getting detailed analytics often requires external tools or API work. The built-in dashboard is described as good for high-level views but insufficient for deep dives or compliance reporting.

Feature Gaps — Integrations & Legacy App Support Moderate pattern Features
▼

Users want better integration with hypervisors, databases, and SaaS apps like Office 365. Active Directory restoration and file-level recovery gaps are specifically called out. Legacy or niche application configuration described as requiring significant manual effort compared to modern virtualized workloads.

"Need for improved integration with hypervisors, databases, and applications like Office 365. Users seek expanded application support including file and active directory restoration."

AI Capabilities — Behind Expectations Emerging pattern AI
▼

Reviewers acknowledge Rubrik is investing in AI but describe the current state as early/initial phase. Users rate Rubrik's AI capabilities around 7/10 and say they're "expecting more." This is notable given Rubrik's heavy AI-focused marketing (SAGE, Ruby AI assistant). The gap between marketing and actual capability is a recurring theme.

Gartner Peer Insights2025
"Regarding Artificial Intelligence, Rubrik is in the initial phase, and reviewers are expecting more from them, giving them seven out of ten for their improvements in that area."
gartner.com — Rubrik Security Cloud likes/dislikes 2025
PeerSpot2025
"Rubrik is actively promoting AI features but users in enterprise environments note the capabilities are still maturing compared to what's been marketed."
